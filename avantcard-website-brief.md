# AvantCard Website Layer / Landing Page Brief

AvantCard should be positioned as a premium identity experience and lead-conversion platform, not merely a digital business card product. The site should communicate that each card combines elegant presentation with practical business value through contact details, intro video, QR access, design personalisation, and a backend lead-monitoring layer similar to a mini CRM. [file:8]

## Brand Positioning

The core message should be that AvantCard transforms a business card from a static contact exchange into a branded, measurable experience. The product story should clearly express four value pillars: premium digital card presentation, rich media support such as intro video and QR, user-controlled design customisation, and a backend intelligence layer for tracking engagement and leads. [file:8]

Recommended positioning lines:

- Your business card is an experience.
- A premium digital card with lead intelligence built in.
- From first impression to follow-up, in one card layer.

## Visual Direction

The visual direction should feel avant-garde, premium, restrained, and executive rather than playful or startup-generic. The attached references suggest a refined black-and-copper identity, and that aligns with the workspace design preference for a clean, minimalist, high-credibility interface with strong hierarchy, modern sans direction, soft surfaces, and controlled use of accent colour. [file:3][file:8]

Recommended design language:

- Primary tones: deep charcoal or near-black.
- Accent tones: copper or cognac metallic-inspired highlights.
- Backgrounds: soft ivory, pearl, or warm white.
- Secondary neutrals: graphite, stone, muted slate.
- Typography: refined serif for selective brand moments, paired with a modern sans for body copy and UI.
- Iconography: classic line icons only.
- Layout: calm, asymmetric, editorial, premium, and spacious.

Avoid loud SaaS tropes such as neon accents, rainbow gradients, glassmorphism overload, emojis, or repetitive three-column feature grids. [file:3]

## Messaging Strategy

The homepage should sell prestige first and intelligence second. The front-end card experience should create desire and memorability, while the backend analytics and mini CRM layer should provide the commercial justification by showing that interactions can be measured, leads monitored, and follow-up improved. [file:8]

Recommended narrative sequence:

1. Define AvantCard as a business card experience, not just a contact file. [file:8]
2. Show the experience layer: contact details, video intro, QR access, and premium presentation. [file:8]
3. Show design control: users can customise the look and feel of the card. [file:8]
4. Reveal the intelligence layer: every interaction can become a measurable lead signal. [file:8]
5. Introduce the mini CRM concept: views, scans, engagement, follow-up, and opportunity tracking. [file:8]
6. End with a strong demo or onboarding call to action. [file:8]

## Section-by-Section Creative Notes

### Header

Keep the header minimal, premium, and sticky. The nav should feel editorial and clean, with links such as Product, Experience, Insights, Pricing, and Contact, plus a primary CTA like Book a Demo.

### Hero

The hero should feel like a product reveal rather than a standard SaaS template. Use a statement such as “Your business card is an experience” supported by copy that explains AvantCard as a digital identity layer combining presentation, media, and measurable lead intelligence. [file:8]

Possible hero copy:

- Headline: Your business card is an experience.
- Alternative headline: First impressions, designed to convert.
- Alternative headline: A digital card with presence — and intelligence.
- Supporting line: AvantCard blends premium digital identity, rich media, QR access, and lead intelligence into one elegant card layer. [file:8]

### Card Experience

This section should showcase the actual card as an immersive interface. Include profile identity, contact details, intro video, QR code, quick actions, and a sense of polished motion or interactivity.

### Design Control

This section should communicate that every card can be styled to reflect a person, brand, or company. Present layout options, theme variations, colour systems, and custom brand expression without making the page feel overloaded.

### Lead Intelligence

This is where the site reveals that AvantCard has operational depth. Show a clean mini CRM interface with metrics such as card opens, profile views, QR scans, leads captured, contact saves, and follow-up status. [file:8]

Suggested section lines:

- Headline: Not just shared. Measured. [file:8]
- Supporting line: Track views, scans, saves, and follow-up opportunities from every card interaction. [file:8]

### How It Works

Use a clean three-step flow:

1. Design your card.
2. Share your experience.
3. Track every lead.

The layout should be elegant and simple, using premium spacing rather than loud visual devices.

### Why AvantCard

Use short, high-value statements focused on business outcomes:

- Stronger brand presence.
- Richer first interactions.
- Measurable engagement.
- Better follow-up visibility.
- A card experience that works as a lead engine.

### Final CTA

The closing section should be visually refined and conversion-oriented rather than aggressive. Recommended CTA line: “Turn every introduction into a trackable opportunity.” [file:8]
